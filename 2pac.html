The ETCH Clock's face consists primarily of elastomer which allows the changing numbers to appear and disappear seamlessly. The aluminum frame around the clock makes it more sturdy and capable of being hung up on a wall.

How to Make Your Own

Don't have the money, but still want the experience? Follow these simple steps!

  1. Purchase some thermoplastic elastomer for cheap from India. Also pick up some aluminum while you're at it.
  2. Construct a metal mold in which to melt down the elastomer. Melt it down.
  3. Cut the aluminum frame out around the elastomer face once it has cooled. Fasten it with whatever bolts.
  4. Do a lot of engineering stuff I don't quite understand.
