Over Half of All Americans Have Cardiac Complications at Some Point in Their Lives

How and why have we as a society become so utterly unhealthy?

Throughout the twentieth and early twent-first centuries, adolescent diabetes has increased eightfold. Obesity has become commonplace within America, and few people think anything of it. As technology continues to increase in complexity, its tendency to distract and detract from people's actual, real lives increases- not linearly, but exponentially. This means: less healthy meals prepared, less exercise, less care for ourselves. If we continue to mistreat our bodies so substantially, it's inevitable that it will come back to bite us in the rear.
